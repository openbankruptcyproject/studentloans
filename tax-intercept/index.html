Treasury Offset Program — Tax Refund Seizure for Student Loan Default

The Treasury Offset Program (TOP) seizes federal tax refunds to apply against defaulted federal student loans. Bankruptcy stops new seizures via the automatic stay; discharge eliminates the underlying debt. This page covers how TOP works, what bankruptcy does to it, and how to recover seized funds when stay violations occur.

How Treasury Offset Program works

Under 31 U.S.C. § 3716 and 31 C.F.R. Part 285, federal agencies refer defaulted debts to the Bureau of the Fiscal Service for offset. When you file a federal tax return seeking a refund, the Bureau matches the debt against the refund and reroutes the refund (in whole or in part) to the agency.

For student loans:

  • Federal tax refund: 100% offset until the debt is paid in full
  • Notice: a 60-day pre-offset notice is generally sent before the first offset
  • EITC and Child Tax Credit portions of the refund: also subject to offset (no protection)
  • Joint filers: the non-debtor spouse can file Form 8379 (Injured Spouse Allocation) to recover their portion

Pandemic pauses and current status

Tax refund offset for federal student loans was suspended during COVID (March 2020 through resumption in 2024). Resumption rolled out in waves through 2024-2025. Borrowers in IDR who would have had $0 IDR payments during the pause are getting credit toward forgiveness; borrowers in default during the pause may be subject to offset on resumption.

Bankruptcy and TOP

The automatic stay stops new offsets

Filing a bankruptcy petition immediately stops new TOP offsets under § 362. Treasury must be notified of the filing — the Bureau of the Fiscal Service requires the debtor or counsel to file a Form 1024 (or notify Treasury directly) to halt active offsets in the system.

If Treasury offsets a refund after the petition is filed, this is generally a stay violation. The debtor can recover under § 362(k) with attorney fees.

Pre-petition offsets are not automatically recoverable

If Treasury offset your refund before you filed bankruptcy, the funds were applied to the debt and are generally not recoverable. The offset is treated as a payment received pre-petition.

Discharge eliminates future offsets

If § 523(a)(8) AP succeeds (or TPD/closed school/false certification discharge), the underlying loan is eliminated and TOP cannot offset future refunds.

Recovering offset funds when discharge succeeds

Some borrowers obtain post-discharge orders directing the Department of Education to refund offset amounts that were applied during the pendency of the bankruptcy. Authority under § 105(a) and § 524(i) is sometimes used. This is fact-specific and not always available; consult counsel.

Practical step: notify Treasury early

Don't rely on Treasury to learn about your bankruptcy filing automatically. File Form 1024 or have counsel notify the Bureau of the Fiscal Service in writing within days of filing. This minimizes the risk of stay-violation offsets that you then have to litigate to recover.
