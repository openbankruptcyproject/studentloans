Administrative Wage Garnishment for Student Loans

Federal student loans in default can be collected through administrative wage garnishment (AWG) without the holder needing a court judgment. The maximum is 15% of disposable income, capped at 30 times the federal minimum wage. Bankruptcy stops AWG immediately under the automatic stay; § 523(a)(8) discharge eliminates the underlying debt.

How administrative wage garnishment works

Under 20 U.S.C. § 1095a, the Department of Education or its guaranty agency can issue a garnishment order to your employer without going through a court. The employer must withhold the specified percentage from each paycheck and remit it to the holder.

Procedural protections (limited but they exist):

  • 30-day pre-garnishment notice (Form FMS-2231)
  • Right to request a hearing on the existence and amount of the debt
  • Right to request a financial-hardship review reducing the percentage
  • Right to a "loan rehabilitation" agreement that stops garnishment after 9 on-time payments

What bankruptcy does to AWG

Filing bankruptcy:

  • Immediately stops new AWG under the automatic stay (§ 362). Your employer must stop withholding upon receiving notice of the filing. The U.S. Trustee fields recover-sought-funds motions if the holder fails to halt.
  • Withheld amounts after the petition filing are recoverable as a stay violation.
  • Withheld amounts before the petition: not automatically recoverable. The holder applied them per the agreement.

Discharge of the underlying loan (via § 523(a)(8) AP or via TPD/closed school/false certification) ends AWG permanently — the debt no longer exists.

Without bankruptcy: stopping AWG

If you don't want to file bankruptcy, you can:

  1. Loan rehabilitation — 9 monthly payments at an income-based amount; restores good-standing and ends AWG.
  2. Loan consolidation — consolidate the defaulted loan into a Direct Consolidation Loan; ends AWG and restores eligibility for IDR/forgiveness.
  3. Hardship reduction — show that 15% creates unmanageable hardship; the holder can lower the percentage.
  4. Disability discharge (TPD) — if eligible, TPD ends the debt entirely.

Bankruptcy is often the fastest stop

For borrowers facing AWG who also have other discharge needs, bankruptcy filing produces the cleanest immediate result. The automatic stay stops AWG the day the petition is filed. Loan-rehabilitation takes 9 months; bankruptcy filing takes a few hours.
